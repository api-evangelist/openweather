Weather is the fundamental driver of the modern energy industry, and a summer heatwave exposes that sharply as millions of cooling systems switch on within hours, tightening the correlation between degrees Celsius and megawatts consumed. The post argues that energy traders and grid operators increasingly rely on precision weather data to understand the mechanisms behind this volatility and achieve greater grid stability and operational efficiency.
