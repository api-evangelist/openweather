Melting roads were long treated as a problem for hot regions like Texas or the Australian outback rather than the temperate north. That assumption broke in July 2022, when the UK passed 40 degrees Celsius for the first time in recorded history, melting road surfaces, damaging runways, and buckling rail lines across the country.
